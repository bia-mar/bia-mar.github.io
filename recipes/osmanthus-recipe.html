桂花糕

Sweet 3h

Osmanthus Jelly (Gui Hua Gao) — a delicate, jewel-toned Chinese jelly perfumed with osmanthus flowers and studded with goji berries.

Servings
10

Ingredients

  • 10 grams dried osmanthus flower tea (pick out any twigs or dark leaves)
  • 20 grams dried wolfberries / goji berries (soaked in hot water)
  • 1 konnyaku jelly powder (or agar agar / gelatine — amount as per packet)
  • 1 litre water
  • 150 grams granulated white sugar (skip if your konnyaku packet already contains sugar)

Recipe

  1. Step 1

    Prep the berries and flowers: Rinse the goji berries and soak them in a bowl of hot water. Remove any hard dark bits (twigs or leaves) from the osmanthus tea — these will make the jelly gritty and bitter.

  2. Step 2

    Brew the osmanthus tea: Heat 1 litre of water in a pot. Just before it boils, add the osmanthus tea and let it simmer for 5 minutes. The longer it steeps, the more intense the flavour and the more golden the colour — but don't overdo it or it turns bitter.

  3. Step 3

    Mix powder and sugar: While the tea simmers, mix the konnyaku jelly powder with the sugar in a bowl until thoroughly combined. This prevents the powder from clumping when added to the hot liquid. (Skip this step if you're using honey or rock sugar instead.)

  4. Step 4

    Add the setting agent: Bring the osmanthus tea back to a full boil, then quickly pour in the powder-sugar mixture while whisking continuously. Reduce to a simmer and whisk for 3–4 minutes until all the powder has fully dissolved and no lumps remain. Skim off any foam.

  5. Step 5

    Add the goji berries: Drain the goji berries — do not add the soaking water as it may affect setting. Add the berries to the pot and simmer for another 3 minutes, then turn off the heat.

  6. Step 6

    Set the jelly: Pour the liquid jelly into silicone moulds or a wide shallow container. Stir each mould gently with a toothpick to distribute the berries and flowers evenly. Leave to cool, then refrigerate for at least 180 minutes, ideally overnight. If using a container, cut into squares with a sharp (non-serrated) knife once set.

Notes and Tips

  1. Recipe & Source:

    greedygirlgourmet.com (opens in new tab)

  2. Safety note:

    Konnyaku jelly is a choking hazard for young children and the elderly — consider using agar agar if serving to them.

  3. Tips:

    Use good quality osmanthus tea — mostly flowers, no branches. The quality makes a noticeable difference in flavour.

  4. Konnyaku powder gives the prettiest, most jewel-toned result; agar agar works well but produces a slightly cloudier jelly.

  5. Check your konnyaku packet — many brands already include sugar, so you may not need to add any.

  6. Do not use more than 40g of goji berries per litre of water or the jelly will taste fibrous and may not set properly.
