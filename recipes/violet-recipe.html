Violettes de Toulouse

Sweet

48h

Crystallised Violets (bonbons) — Delicate sugar-coated violets, dried until crisp. A fragrant 19th-century treat for decorating cakes and tarts.

Servings
2 or used as garnish

Ingredients

  • 1 egg white
  • 100 grams caster sugar
  • 1 handful of fresh violets (picked from a pollution-free area)

Recipe

  1. Step 1

    Pick and clean the violets: Pick a handful of fragrant fresh violets — the more scented, the better. Remove the stems as close to the flower as possible. Gently dip them in cold water, then spread them out open-faced between two sheets of paper towel. Repeat until the paper stays dry.

  2. Step 2

    Beat the egg white: Lightly beat 1 egg white with a fork — just enough to loosen it. Do not whip it into stiff peaks, as the sugar won't stick if it's over-beaten.

  3. Step 3

    Coat with egg white: Using a fine brush, gently apply the egg white to each violet, keeping the petals open and separated. This method (vs. dipping) helps prevent the petals from sticking together.

  4. Step 4

    Sugar the violets: Place each coated violet into a bowl filled with caster sugar and sprinkle sugar generously over it until fully coated. Don't be shy with the sugar — the excess will fall off once dry.

  5. Step 5

    Dry: Lay the violets well spaced on a baking tray lined with parchment or a silicone sheet. Place in a sunny spot if possible. They are ready when completely hard and crisp — about 120 minutes in the sun.

  6. Step 6

    Store: Store in a jar or airtight container. The excess sugar that collects at the bottom is wonderfully fragrant — use it to flavour plain yoghurt.

Notes and Tips

  1. Recipe & Source:

    luckysophie.com

  2. Fun fact:

    Violettes de Toulouse have been made since the 19th century and became the symbol of the city!

  3. Tips:

    Use violets picked on a dry day — flowers picked after rain tend to be damp and less fragrant, and won't crystallise as well.

  4. These are best used as decoration for chocolate tarts, ganaches, or celebration cakes.
