Sesbania Flower Spicy Salad — a fresh and vibrant Thai salad with edible sesbania flowers, shrimp and minced chicken in a tangy fish sauce dressing.

Servings
2

Ingredients

  • 250 grams sesbania grandiflora flowers (agati / dok kae)
  • 6 shrimp, peeled
  • 100 grams minced chicken breast
  • 2 bird's eye chilies, chopped (or to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1.5 tablespoons sugar
  • 1.5 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 4 shallots (or ¼ onion), thinly sliced
  • 1 handful of tomatoes, halved
  • 1 spring onion, chopped
  • Fresh coriander, chopped

Recipe

  1. Step 1

    Wash the flowers: Rinse the sesbania flowers thoroughly under cold water and set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Blanch the flowers: Bring a pot of water to the boil. Blanch the flowers briefly until just wilted. Drain and set aside.

  3. Step 3

    Blanch the shrimp: In the same or a fresh pot of boiling water, blanch the shrimp until pink and just cooked through. Drain and set aside.

  4. Step 4

    Blanch the chicken: Blanch the minced chicken in boiling water, breaking it up as it cooks, until fully cooked. Drain and set aside.

  5. Step 5

    Make the dressing: In a large bowl, mix chopped chilies, fish sauce, lime juice and sugar together. Taste and adjust the balance of spicy, sour, salty and sweet to your liking.

  6. Step 6

    Toss the salad: Add the blanched flowers, shrimp, chicken, tomatoes and shallots to the dressing. Toss well to coat everything evenly.

  7. Step 7

    Garnish and serve: Top with chopped spring onions and fresh coriander. Serve immediately.

Notes and Tips

  1. Recipe & Source:

    Cookpad Thailand

  2. Tips:

    The dressing balance is key — start with the lower amounts of fish sauce and sugar, then adjust to taste.

  3. Shallots are traditional but regular onion works fine.

  4. The flowers should be blanched briefly — just enough to soften, not so long they lose their texture.
